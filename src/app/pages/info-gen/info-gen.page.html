Welcome

to the "Regional Screening Levels for Chemical Contaminants at Superfund Sites (RSL)" and "Regional Removal Management Level (RML)" screening level app. This app was developed with DOE's Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) under an Interagency Agreement. The RSLs are now the source of screening levels for all the EPA regions. The RSLs and RMLs provide comparison values for residential and commercial/industrial exposures to soil, air, and tapwater (drinking water). The unified use of the RSLs, to screen chemicals at Superfund sites, promotes national consistency. The RSLs and RMLs are calculated with the same models using the latest toxicity values, default exposure assumptions, and physical and chemical properties. To ensure proper use of the RSLs, review the What's New, User's Guide, and Frequent Questions. To ensure proper use of the RMLs, review the User's Guide and Frequent Questions. There is no What's New page for the RML. The changes to the What's New are synchronous with the RSL What's New.
